# Ongera Ubeho — Resolution Project SVC Pitch (5:00 total)

**Format:** 12 slides · ~25 seconds each · judges stop you at exactly 5:00.
**Presenting:** run `npm run dev` (or open the live deck), press `F` for fullscreen, `P` for presenter mode (timer + these notes + next-slide preview). Arrow keys advance — each press reveals the next build *within* a slide before moving on.

> Judges score the **venture** (community impact, youth-led, lasting organization, team, goals, credible budget, allies, ethics) *and* the **founders** (integrity, poise, initiative, organization, charisma, teamwork, coachability). Slides 2–3 exist for the founder criteria; slides 8 and 10 exist for the "Ally" and "lasting organization" criteria.

---

**1 · Cover (0:00–0:25).** Muraho — good morning. Introduce all three founders by name. "Ongera Ubeho means *Live More — Choose Life* in Kinyarwanda." One breath, then advance.

**2 · Our story (0:25–0:55).** The forum brief says: *present yourself and your connection to the issue first.* Speak personally — classmates lost, parents with nowhere to turn. Land the quote: "Our communities held us. Now it is our turn to hold others." Builds: 4 clicks.

**3 · Team (0:55–1:20).** One line each: Macrine — strategy & community (SANGA INSHUTI); Serge — the USSD/WhatsApp platform; Henriette — operations (Solid Minds). Say "youth-created, youth-led, women-led" out loud — it is a scored criterion.

**4 · The problem (1:20–1:55).** Numbers first, then the cascade (1 click), then the parent line (1 click). ⚠️ **Before the pitch: verify the three statistics** (7,000+ street children; 6,200+ in addiction centres; 776 dropouts) and be ready to name the source in Q&A — Rwanda Biomedical Centre / National Rehabilitation Service, NISR, MINEDUC year and report.

**5 · The gap (1:55–2:25).** Walk the left column of the table. Key contrast: 2,000–5,000 RWF vs 20,000+; any phone vs smartphone; Kinyarwanda vs clinical English.

**6 · Solution (2:25–3:00).** Two clicks — one per track — then the referral line and the thesis: "Technology supports people — it never replaces them."

**7 · Who we serve (3:00–3:30).** Be specific: youth 12–25, Kigali first. Numbers: 50 / 30 / 40 / 10 = 130+ direct beneficiaries, 60%+ women & girls.

**8 · Traction & allies (3:30–4:00).** 4 clicks. The HATANA win is *evidence*, said once, professionally. Point at the live site in the frame. Name the allies — judges score "Involves an Ally" explicitly.

**9 · Measuring impact (4:00–4:25).** "Measured, not claimed." Baseline + follow-up for every participant; 80% / 70% / 60% targets; M&E is a budget line.

**10 · Sustainability (4:25–4:50 — keep brisk).** Resolution funds once: fees subsidise street youth, plus grants, government, CSR. Say "documented curriculum and a transition plan — the venture outlives us." Then the timeline: 6 months → 1 year → 2 years → 5 years.

**11 · Budget (4:50 — 10 seconds only).** "Five thousand dollars, one time, fully allocated — every line buys something you can count." Do NOT read the rows; they're for Q&A.

**12 · Close (final 10 seconds).** Three lines, the invitation, "Murakoze." Leave contact details on screen through Q&A.

## Q&A preparation (judges get 5–10 min)
- **Goals by horizon** (they will ask): 6 months = pilot complete, evidence base; 1 year = fee revenue live, second cohort; 2 years = district replication with MINISANTE/MIGEPROF.
- **Child safeguarding**: mentors vetted and trained, escalation protocol to professional services (Solid Minds link), referral to government shelters — never handle clinical cases ourselves.
- **Why will mentors stay?** They are paid, trained, certified — a respected community role.
- **What if families can't pay?** Fully subsidised; fees from those who can pay cross-subsidise.
- **USSD cost?** Platform line ($1,200) covers development + session costs for the pilot; telco partnership is a Year-1 target.
