The Challenge

Youth are falling through the cracks

Across Rwanda, young people are drifting into addiction, gambling, and the streets — not because families do not care, but because when the warning signs appear, there is nowhere affordable, nearby, and in their own language to turn.

  • Professional counselling costs 20,000+ RWF per session — and exists mainly in cities.
  • Street children have no structured pathway back to family and school.
  • Parents watch problems grow in silence, with no one to guide them.

What We Do

One hub. Two connected tracks.

A physical safe space combined with a simple digital system — built on human connection first, because Rwandans trust community-based, face-to-face relationships more than apps alone.

Track 1

Family Mentorship

Parents and youth still at home are matched with a trained community mentor — by basic phone or WhatsApp — before the crisis becomes irreversible.

Explore Track 1
Track 2

Street Youth Rehabilitation

Weekly outreach builds trust with street youth, then offers food, a safe space, life skills, and a structured route back to family and dignity.
